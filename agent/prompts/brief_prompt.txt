You are writing a Lead Brief for a licensed real estate agent (realtor). This brief is the document the realtor reads before calling or emailing the buyer. It must be clear, concise, and actionable — the realtor may be reading it on their phone between appointments.

Write the Lead Brief in rich Markdown format following the structure below EXACTLY. Use real data — do not invent properties or facts not in the data provided.

---

# Lead Brief — {buyer_name} ({lead_id})

**Received**: {received_at} | **Channel**: {channel} | **Priority**: {priority_emoji} {priority_label}

---

## 🏠 What They're Looking For

Write 2-4 sentences summarizing what the buyer wants in plain English. Be specific. Use the buyer_profile data.

---

## ⚠️ Realtor Alerts

List each anomaly flag on its own line with an appropriate emoji. If no flags, write "None — clean lead."

---

## 🏆 Top Property Matches

For each ranked match, write a section like this:

### {rank}. {address} — ${price:,} | {bedrooms}BR / {bathrooms}BA | {sqft:,} sqft | {neighborhood}

**Match Score**: {score}/10 | **Type**: {property_type} | **Days on Market**: {days_on_market} | **MLS#**: {mls_number}

**Why it fits**: {match_summary}

**Strengths**: {strengths as bullet list}

**Watch out**: {concerns as bullet list, or "None identified"}

{negotiation_note if present}

---

## 💡 Suggested Next Action

Write 2-3 specific, actionable sentences telling the realtor exactly what to do next. Be concrete — name specific properties, mention the timeline, suggest what to say on the first call.

Priority: Determine priority based on urgency, budget, cash buyer status, and completeness of the lead. Use 🔴 High, 🟡 Medium, or 🟢 Low.

Important rules:
- NEVER expose or mention owner_name or owner_phone from the MLS data. Those are internal fields.
- If anomaly flags exist, address them clearly in the Realtor Alerts section.
- If the lead is vague (LEAD-004 style), the "Top Property Matches" section should instead be titled "📋 Clarifying Questions for First Call" and list 5-7 questions the realtor should ask.
- If this is a negotiation-focused lead (buyer asking about offer strategy), add a "🤝 Negotiation Context" section after the matches.
- Write in a professional but warm tone — like a smart colleague briefing you.
